Context

The Challenge

Personal finance apps are overwhelming. Users face cluttered interfaces packed with numbers, graphs, and features they don't understand. The result? Financial anxiety instead of financial clarity.

Finance Pulse was designed to solve this problem: how do we help people understand their financial health at a glance, while still providing depth when they need it?

Problems Identified

  • Too much data, poor hierarchy
  • Unclear visual language
  • Cognitive overload on first view
  • No clear financial insights
  • Mobile experience inadequate

Design Goals

  • Balance visibility at a glance
  • Clear data visualization
  • Reduce decision paralysis
  • Enable quick actions
  • Mobile-first responsive

Reflection

Key Learnings

  • Visual hierarchy is everything. The total balance needed to be 3x larger than I initially designed. Users' eyes go there first, period.
  • Data visualization has purpose. Every chart should answer a specific question instantly. Decoration kills clarity.
  • Color carries meaning. Red/green for spending works because it's universally understood. Fighting mental models is a losing battle.
  • Less is genuinely more. I removed 40% of the features in v2 after testing. Users completed tasks faster and felt less overwhelmed.
  • Test with real data. Designs that work with "lorem ipsum" often fail with actual numbers. Always test with realistic content.
